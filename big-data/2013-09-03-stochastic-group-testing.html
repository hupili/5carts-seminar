Title: Stochastic Threshold Group Testing

Authors: Chun Lam Chan, Sheng Cai, Mayank Bakshi, Sidharth Jaggi, Vankatesh Saligrama

Abstract: We formulate and analyze a stochastic threshold group testing problem motivated by biological applications. Here a set of $n$ items contains a subset of $d \ll n$ defective items. Subsets (pools) of the $n$ items are tested. The test outcomes are negative if the number of defectives in a pool is no larger than $l$; positive if the pool contains more than $u$ defectives, and stochastic (negative/positive with some probability) if the number of defectives in the pool is in the interval $[l,\,u]$. The goal of our {\it stochastic threshold group testing} scheme is to identify the set of $d$ defective items via a ``small'' number of such tests with high probability. In the regime that $l = o({d})$ we present schemes that are computationally feasible to design and implement, and require near-optimal number of tests. Our schemes are robust to a variety of models for probabilistic threshold group testing.
